Carl Edward Sagan

"Relativity does set limits on what humans can ultimately do. But the universe is not required to be in perfect harmony with human aspirations."

A Brief History Of Carl Sagan:

  • 1934 - Born in Brooklyn, New York on November 9 to Samuel Sagan, a Russian immigrant, and Rachel Molly Gruber of New York.
  • 1939 - Visited the 1939 New York World's Fair with his parents, a trip which Sagan later recalled being one of his most defining memories.
  • 1942 - Took regular trips to the American Museum of Natural History across the East River in Manhattan with a close friend.
  • 1947 - Discovered Astounding Science Fiction magazine, which helped develop his inquisitiveness.
  • 1948 - Moved to the nearby town of Rahway, New Jersey to enter Rahway High School, where he was a straight A student bored by unchallenging classes and uninspiring teachers.
  • 1951 - Attended the University of Chicago, where he participated in the Ryerson Astronomical Society.
  • 1955 - Finished a B.S. degree in physics, paving the way for an M.S. degree in physics the following year.
  • 1958 - Advised the newly estabilished NASA on the inception of the U.S. space program.
  • 1960 - Earned a Ph.D degree with his dissertation "Physical Studies of Planets" submitted to the Department of Astronomy and Astrophysics.
  • 1962 - Began working at the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory in Cambridge, Massachusetts.
  • 1966 - Received national attention with his book "Intelligent Life in the Universe."
  • 1967 - Wrote an article in National Geographic on the potential of life on other planets.
  • 1968 - Moved to Cornell University in Ithaca New York, where he became a full time professor three years later; directed the school's Laboratory for Planetary Studies.
  • 1969 Briefed Apollo 11 astronauts before their flight to the Moon.
  • 1972 - Conceived and assembled the first physical message sent into space; a gold-anodized plaque attached to the space probe Pioneer 10.
  • 1974 - Helped Dr. Frank Drake craft a radio message sent into space from the Arecibo radio telescope, which was aimed at informing extraterrestrials about Earth.
  • 1977 - Gave a series of speeches as part of the "Royal Institution Christmas Lectures" in London.
  • 1980 - Hosted, co-wrote and co-produced the thirteen-part PBS television series "Cosmos: A Personal Voyage".
  • 1982 - Formed a petition signed by 70 scientists advocating SETI(Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence), which was published in the journal Science.
  • 1983 - Joined an extensive scientific study of the atmospheric consequences of nuclear war; introduced the idea of a "Nuclear Winter".
  • 1985 - Wrote the book "Contact".
  • 1994 - Published his famous reflections on the Pale Blue Dot, an image of Earth taken by Voyager 1 on Febriary 14, 1990.
  • 1996 - Died of pneumonia on December 20 after suffering from a rare bone marrow disease; he was just 62.
  • 1997 - The movie Contact, based on Sagan's 1985 novel of the same name, ended with the dedication "For Carl".
  • 2004 - Steve Squyres, a former student of Sagan, led the team that landed the rovers Spirit and Opportunity on Mars.

"Somewhere, something incredible is waiting to be known."
